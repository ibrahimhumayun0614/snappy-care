Privacy Policy

Our commitment to your privacy is unwavering. This document outlines how we protect and manage your data.

Contents

1. Data Controller 2. Info We Collect 3. How We Collect 4. Legal Basis 5. How We Use 6. Cookies 7. How We Share 8. Data Retention 9. Your Rights 10. Children's Data 11. Policy Changes 12. Contact us
Last Updated: April 20, 2026

Snappy Care (“we”, “our”, or “us”) is committed to protecting your privacy and personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information when you use our website, mobile services, and home healthcare services.

By using Snappy Care services, you agree to the practices described in this Policy.

1. Data Controller

Snappy Care acts as the data controller for your personal data.

For any questions regarding this Privacy Policy or your data rights, you can contact us using the details in the “Contact Us” section.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal data:

2.1 Personal Identification Data

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Residential address

2.2 Health and Care-Related Information (Sensitive Data)

As a healthcare/home-care provider, we may collect:

  • Patient care requirements
  • Medical conditions (as shared by you or authorized persons)
  • Care plans and instructions
  • Emergency contact details
  • Special needs or mobility information

This data is treated as sensitive personal data and is handled with enhanced security and confidentiality.

2.3 Demographic Information

  • Age
  • Gender (if provided for care purposes)

2.4 Technical and Usage Data

  • IP address
  • Device information
  • Browser type
  • Website usage behavior

3. How We Collect Information

We collect information in the following ways:

3.1 Directly from You

When you:

  • Book a caregiver or service
  • Fill out forms or inquiries
  • Contact our support team
  • Register on our platform

3.2 From Care Interactions

When caregivers provide services, they may record service-related updates or care notes necessary for continuity of care.

3.3 Automatically

Through:

  • Cookies
  • Analytics tools
  • Website tracking technologies

3.4 From Authorized Third Parties

We may receive information from:

  • Family members or guardians
  • Healthcare professionals (when authorized)
  • Partners or franchise caregivers

4. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

We process your data based on:

  • Consent – when you agree to care services or communications
  • Contractual necessity – to deliver home healthcare services
  • Legal obligations – compliance with healthcare and safety laws
  • Vital interests – in emergency or health-critical situations
  • Legitimate interests – improving service quality and operations

5. How We Use Your Information

We use your data to:

5.1 Provide Healthcare Services

  • Assign caregivers
  • Deliver home healthcare and support services
  • Manage care schedules
  • Maintain care continuity

5.2 Communication

  • Service confirmations and updates
  • Emergency notifications
  • Care coordination between caregivers and family members

5.3 Service Improvement

  • Monitor service quality
  • Train caregivers
  • Improve user experience

5.4 Safety and Security

  • Protect patients and caregivers
  • Prevent misuse or fraud
  • Respond to emergencies

5.5 Legal and Regulatory Compliance

  • Meet healthcare-related legal obligations
  • Maintain required service records

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Improve website functionality
  • Analyze usage trends
  • Enhance user experience

You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but some features may not function properly.

7. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your information only when necessary with:

7.1 Caregivers and Medical Staff

To deliver scheduled home healthcare services.

7.2 Authorized Family Members or Guardians

When required for care coordination or emergencies.

7.3 Service Providers

Third parties who assist in:

  • Technology infrastructure
  • Payment processing
  • Customer support

7.4 Legal Authorities

When required by law or to protect safety and rights.

7.5 Business Transfers

In case of merger, acquisition, or restructuring.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal and healthcare-related data only as long as necessary to:

  • Provide care services
  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Resolve disputes
  • Maintain safety records

After this period, data is securely deleted or anonymized.

9. Your Data Protection Rights (GDPR)

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Restrict or object to processing
  • Withdraw consent at any time
  • Request data portability

To exercise these rights, please contact us.

10. Children’s and Dependent Care Data

Snappy Care may process data relating to children or dependent individuals only with consent from a parent, guardian, or authorized representative.

We handle such data with enhanced protection measures.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised “Last Updated” date.
